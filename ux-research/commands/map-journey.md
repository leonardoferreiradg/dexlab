# /map-journey

Map the experience as users actually live it — not as the product team imagines it.

## Steps

1. **Define the journey scope** — Which user, which scenario, which start and end points? A journey without a defined scope maps everything and informs nothing.

2. **Map the journey** — Use `uxr_journey-map` to document: phases, user actions, touchpoints (product and non-product), system responses, and emotional state at each step. Sources: research transcripts, observation notes, support ticket patterns.

3. **Validate emotional data** — Use `uxr_empathy-map` to cross-check the emotional arc. Where do users feel anxious? Relieved? Frustrated? Surprised? The emotional arc is not decorative — it reveals where the product creates or destroys value.

4. **Annotate opportunities** — For every pain point: frame it as a design opportunity. Format: "Users feel [emotion] when [situation] because [cause]. Opportunity: [direction]." Every opportunity must point to a cause, not just the symptom.

5. **Prioritize** — Score opportunities: user impact × frequency × feasibility. Surface the top 3 for design exploration.

## Output

Journey map with phases, touchpoints, emotional arc, pain point annotations, and a prioritized opportunity list with evidence citations.

## Follow-up

Use `/product_frame-problem` to translate journey opportunities into a design brief, or `/audit_map-flow` to validate the current product flow against the journey.
