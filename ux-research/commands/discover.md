# /discover

Structured discovery from zero to research-informed design direction.

## Steps

1. **Plan the research** — Use `uxr-usability-plan` to define: what questions need answers, which methods match those questions, participant criteria, and timeline. Don't default to "5 user interviews" — the method must match the question.

2. **Create the interview guide** — Use `uxr-interview-script` to write the script: warm-up questions, core topic exploration with open-ended prompts, and wrap-up. Every question must be open-ended. No yes/no questions.

3. **Map JTBD** — Use `uxr-jtbd` to frame the research around jobs: functional, emotional, and social dimensions of what users are trying to accomplish. Jobs persist longer than feature preferences.

4. **Build empathy map** — Use `uxr-empathy-map` to synthesize findings into the Says / Thinks / Does / Feels quadrants. Identify tensions — gaps between what users say and what they do are the most valuable insights.

5. **Map the experience** — Use `uxr-journey-map` to produce the end-to-end journey: phases, touchpoints, emotional arc, pain points, and opportunities. Every opportunity area must be traced to research evidence.

## Output

Research plan, interview guide, JTBD map, empathy map, and user journey with annotated opportunity areas. Evidence-based foundation for every design decision that follows.

## Follow-up

Use `/product-frame-problem` to translate research opportunities into a design brief.
